Pop Eye Treatment

Comprehensive guide to identifying and curing Pop Eye.

One or both eyes protrude or bulge outward due to fluid buildup.

  • Bulging, cloudy, or swollen eyes.
  • Redness behind the eye (trauma).
  • Hiding or erratic swimming.

Move the fish to a hospital tank to treat safely and prevent spreading infection.

Pop Eye is often caused by poor water. Ensure 0 Ammonia/Nitrite and low Nitrate.

Treat based on the cause (Bacterial or Internal).

For Bacterial Infection:

Use Maracyn (Erythromycin) or Melafix.

For Swelling (Epsom Salt):

Add Epsom Salt (1 tsp per 5 gallons) to reduce fluid buildup.
