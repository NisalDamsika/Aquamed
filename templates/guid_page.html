Fish Care Guide

Essential tips to keep your aquarium healthy and thriving.

1. Tank Setup

Size Matters

Choose a tank size based on the species. Larger tanks (20+ gallons) are more stable.

Example: Goldfish need at least 20 gallons for one fish.

Cycling the Tank

Cycle the tank for 4–6 weeks to build beneficial bacteria before adding fish.

Filtration

Use a quality filter rated for your tank size.

  • Fluval: Premium canister filters.
  • AquaClear: Versatile media options.
  • Tetra Whisper: Quiet, beginner-friendly.

2. Water Quality

Dechlorinate Water

Tap water contains toxic chlorine. Always use a conditioner.

  • ✅ Seachem Prime (Highly Recommended)
  • ✅ API Tap Water Conditioner

Test Regularly

Use test kits to monitor pH, ammonia, and nitrates.

Recommended: API Master Test Kit.

3. Feeding

Feed only what fish can eat in 2–3 minutes. Don't overfeed!

Recommended Brands

  • Hikari: High-quality, species-specific.
  • Tetra: Flakes and pellets.
  • New Life Spectrum: Color-enhancing.

4. Temp & Maintenance

Heater

Maintain stable temp (24–27°C for tropical fish). Brands: Eheim Jäger, Fluval E Series.

Cleaning

  • Clean filter monthly (rinse in tank water).
  • Remove algae with magnetic scrapers.
  • Trim live plants regularly.

Avoid Mistakes

  • Don't mix aggressive and peaceful species.
  • Don’t add too many fish at once.
  • Quarantine new fish for 2 weeks.

Starter Kit (Beginners)

  • ✅ 20-gallon tank
  • ✅ Sponge or Hang-on-back filter
  • ✅ Heater & Thermometer
  • ✅ Seachem Prime & API Test Kit
